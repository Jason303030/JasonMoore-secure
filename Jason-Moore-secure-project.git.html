Program Security (ie.viruses,”bad” programming, …)

There are necessary steps that need to be taken in order to keep yourself and your company secure. There are many types of security breaches which may leave you and your company vulnerable to such as errors and viruses. There are steps in order to take which will help to keep you secure from these breeches which may have been caused by bad programming.

Problems:

  • Bad programming or coding may leave you vulnerable to viruses, worms or trojan horses.
  • Bad programming on video games may leave the game open to exploitation and cheating.
  • Not all programmers create good code so there must be a process of protecting the good code from the bad code.
  • Keeping all programming’s from having flaws.
  • We must be able to protect companies’ resources from infiltration.
  • Not all programmers are able to detect bad code or risks from outside influence.
  • We all may access situations in different ways and may not understand the risks of bad coding.
  • New ways of breeching programs may be introduced which programmers may not know how to defend against.
  • Buffer overflow.

Prevention

  • Keep evaluating and maintaining your programs.
  • Keep programmers educated about new exploitations and vulnerabilities.
  • Test the proof of security of your programs.
  • Fix problems quickly.
  • Consider all dimensions of risks.
  • Preventers and creators must work together closely in order to keep security up to date.
  • Address all vulnerable components, open source programs components within the organization.
  • Don’t connect unnecessary programs to the internet.
